Monday Begins on Saturday

Previous Next

Technical Details
iOS icon
Tools Used: Adobe Photoshop
Duration: 3 hours
CMS: No

“Monday Begins on Saturday” is a 1964 science fiction novel by Boris and Arkady Strugatsky. It offers an idealistic view of the scientific work ethic, as reflected in the title, which suggests that the scientists’ weekends are nonexistent. What if this book had an iOS icon? How would it look like? I decided to make one just for kicks.

The Icon

In short, it’s a calendar that shows the day “Saturday – суббота” in Russian, crossed over and below it in red: “Monday – понедельник”, thus making refernce to the title of the book.

Monday Begins on Saturday Icon

The Scientific Research Institute of Sorcery and Wizardry where Sasha — the main character — works, is abbreviated “NIIChaVo” in Russian which sounds very close to “Ничего”, the word for “nothing”. This is reflected at the top of the calendar icon, suggesting that this calendar belongs to/can be found in this Institute.

I had many ideas — some very complex — for this concept but in the end I decided I wanted to make something simple, but beautiful enough. The icon has a 3D feel, with the light coming from the top and the large shadow falloff, almost as if it’s floating in mid-air.
