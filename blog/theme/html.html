The lost art of Pricing

Yesterday, 23/08/2012, Facebook updated their iOS app. My Twitter feed was almost infested with this subject, so much so that I decided to test it out. Tried it on my iPhone 3G but it wouldn’t allow me to update it, so I could only test it on my iPad 2. What’s changed? Well, I’ll start with the improvements they’ve made. They ditched HTML5 for the app and used the native, objective-C language to write it (surprise, surprise!), thus making it very fast and snappy. Also, now they’re caching previous posts and showing them whilst you’re waiting for the app to load new posts. Groundbreaking stuff! Twitter has been doing that since what? 2009?. That’s about it on the improvements; on to the negatives.

Disclaimer: I am in no position to offer you financial advice. I am not a financial advisor. What I want to do, is share my personal experiences on freelancing for a living. This blog is tailored to suit freelancers, so if you’re in a different situation — applying for a full time job, for example — the techniques implemented should be different. That being said, it doesn’t mean that you can’t take away something from this article: useful techniques to tip the scale in your favour.
